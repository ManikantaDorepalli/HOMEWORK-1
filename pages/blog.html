Excerpt from one of my favourite story: To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

To Kill a Mockingbird" explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and empathy in a small Southern town during the 1930s. Through the eyes of young Scout Finch, readers witness her father, Atticus Finch, defending a black man wrongly accused of raping a white woman. The story challenges societal norms and prejudices, emphasizing the importance of compassion and understanding.
